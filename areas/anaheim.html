When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Anaheim

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in Anaheim, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The Anaheim Residential Market

Anaheim's residential base spans nearly a century of construction, from 1920s bungalows in the Colony Historic District to postwar tracts near Disneyland and newer hillside custom homes in Anaheim Hills. Values move sharply from the flatland neighborhoods of the west and central city to the elevated, view-oriented properties east of the 55 freeway. Demand is driven by proximity to the resort district, major employers, and freeway access along the 5, 91, and 57. For a retrospective estate valuation, an appraiser must pair the decedent's property with sales that closed near the date of death within the correct submarket, since a single citywide figure would misstate value in either direction.

Valuation Considerations

Anaheim estate assignments range from modest west-side condominiums to gated hillside estates in Anaheim Hills, so the value spread is wide and the choice of comparable sales is critical. Historic-district overlays, hillside lot premiums, and HOA obligations in the eastern tracts all bear on a defensible date-of-death conclusion.

Frequently Asked Questions — Anaheim

How much does a date-of-death appraisal cost in Anaheim?

How long does a Anaheim date-of-death appraisal take?

Most desktop appraisals for Anaheim properties are completed within three to five business days of order confirmation. Rush delivery is available for time-sensitive estate matters.

Do you need to visit the property in Anaheim?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to Anaheim and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in Anaheim?

Yes. A desktop appraisal prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser to USPAP standards is accepted by the IRS for estate tax filings, stepped-up basis documentation, and Form 706 submissions.

What if the date of death in Anaheim was years ago?

Retrospective appraisals with effective dates well in the past are routine. Historical MLS data and public records let us establish fair market value for a Anaheim property as of a specific prior date with confidence.
