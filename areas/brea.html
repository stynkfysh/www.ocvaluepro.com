When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Brea

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in Brea, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The Brea Residential Market

Brea's housing ranges from older residences near the revitalized Downtown to the newer master-planned homes of Blackstone and La Floresta and the custom properties tucked into the northern hills. Because these segments differ markedly in age, lot character, and price, comparable selection must respect the subject's specific area. For a retrospective valuation as of a date of death, sales from the matching neighborhood and time frame provide the most reliable support, particularly for the individualized homes of Olinda Village and Carbon Canyon. Price levels have been steady, aided by the schools and the city's employment base. The analysis fixes value at the historical date rather than under present conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions — Brea

How much does a date-of-death appraisal cost in Brea?

Desktop date-of-death appraisals for Brea properties start at $299 for the Basic Desktop report and $449 for the Standard Desktop report. Complex or high-value properties may be quoted higher; your exact fee is confirmed before any work begins.

How long does a Brea date-of-death appraisal take?

Most desktop appraisals for Brea properties are completed within three to five business days of order confirmation. Rush delivery is available for time-sensitive estate matters.

Do you need to visit the property in Brea?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to Brea and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in Brea?

Yes. A desktop appraisal prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser to USPAP standards is accepted by the IRS for estate tax filings, stepped-up basis documentation, and Form 706 submissions.

What if the date of death in Brea was years ago?

Retrospective appraisals with effective dates well in the past are routine. Historical MLS data and public records let us establish fair market value for a Brea property as of a specific prior date with confidence.
