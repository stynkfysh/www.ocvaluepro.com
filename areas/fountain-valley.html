When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Fountain Valley

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in Fountain Valley, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The Fountain Valley Residential Market

The city is dominated by single-story and two-story tract homes from the late 1960s and 1970s, many on generous lots, with pockets of townhomes and gated communities such as Tiburon. Strong, stable demand is driven by low crime, well-regarded schools, and the recreational draw of Mile Square Regional Park, keeping price levels solidly in the upper-suburban tier. Because the housing stock is relatively uniform, comparable selection for a retrospective valuation turns on square footage, single- versus two-story configuration, degree of updating, and lot position. Long-held estate properties are common, so distinguishing original condition from renovation as of the date of death is often the pivotal issue.

Valuation Considerations

Given the uniformity of the tract housing, an accurate date-of-death value depends on matching the subject to closely comparable sales near the effective date, which supports a reliable stepped-up basis and any estate tax reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions — Fountain Valley

How much does a date-of-death appraisal cost in Fountain Valley?

How long does a Fountain Valley date-of-death appraisal take?

Most desktop appraisals for Fountain Valley properties are completed within three to five business days of order confirmation. Rush delivery is available for time-sensitive estate matters.

Do you need to visit the property in Fountain Valley?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to Fountain Valley and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in Fountain Valley?

Yes. A desktop appraisal prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser to USPAP standards is accepted by the IRS for estate tax filings, stepped-up basis documentation, and Form 706 submissions.

What if the date of death in Fountain Valley was years ago?

Retrospective appraisals with effective dates well in the past are routine. Historical MLS data and public records let us establish fair market value for a Fountain Valley property as of a specific prior date with confidence.
