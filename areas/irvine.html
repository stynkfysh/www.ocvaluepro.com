When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Irvine

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in Irvine, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The Irvine Residential Market

Irvine's housing stock is unusually planned, moving from the mature 1970s tracts of Woodbridge and University Park to the newer villages rising around the Great Park. Because the Irvine Company built out the city in deliberate phases, comparable evidence is often organized by village and product line rather than by a single citywide trend. For a retrospective valuation, isolating sales that closed near the date of death within the same village and HOA is essential, since price movement in newer Mello-Roos districts has not always tracked the older core. Demand from professionals and international buyers has historically kept this a higher-priced market, though pace and pricing vary by product type. Reconstructing the correct as-of-date requires attention to which phases were actively selling at that moment.

Valuation Considerations

Many Irvine villages carry both HOA dues and Mello-Roos special taxes that differ sharply by phase, so a date-of-death appraisal must confirm the assessments in force as of that date and reflect how they influenced value within the specific village.

How long does a Irvine date-of-death appraisal take?

Do you need to visit the property in Irvine?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to Irvine and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in Irvine?

What if the date of death in Irvine was years ago?
