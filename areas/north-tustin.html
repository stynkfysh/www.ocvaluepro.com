When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in North Tustin

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in North Tustin, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The North Tustin Residential Market

Because North Tustin lies outside any city and consists largely of custom homes, sales are less frequent and less uniform than in surrounding master-planned areas. Lot size, view, topography, and the degree of updating drive value more than any single neighborhood trend. For a retrospective assignment, the limited number of comparable sales near the date of death means each one must be examined closely and adjusted with care. This has long been a higher-priced, low-turnover market where individual property characteristics dominate. Reconstructing value as of the date of death rewards local knowledge of the hillside's micro-markets.

Frequently Asked Questions — North Tustin

How much does a date-of-death appraisal cost in North Tustin?

Desktop date-of-death appraisals for North Tustin properties start at $299 for the Basic Desktop report and $449 for the Standard Desktop report. Complex or high-value properties may be quoted higher; your exact fee is confirmed before any work begins.

How long does a North Tustin date-of-death appraisal take?

Most desktop appraisals for North Tustin properties are completed within three to five business days of order confirmation. Rush delivery is available for time-sensitive estate matters.

Do you need to visit the property in North Tustin?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to North Tustin and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in North Tustin?

Yes. A desktop appraisal prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser to USPAP standards is accepted by the IRS for estate tax filings, stepped-up basis documentation, and Form 706 submissions.

What if the date of death in North Tustin was years ago?

Retrospective appraisals with effective dates well in the past are routine. Historical MLS data and public records let us establish fair market value for a North Tustin property as of a specific prior date with confidence.
