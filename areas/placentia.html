When You Need a Date-of-Death Appraisal in Placentia

A date-of-death appraisal determines the fair market value of real property as of the date its owner passed away. For an estate holding property in Placentia, that value is used for the IRS step-up in basis, Form 706 estate-tax filings, trust distributions among beneficiaries, and probate accounting. Every report is a USPAP-compliant valuation prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser and accepted by the IRS, the courts, and estate professionals.

The Placentia Residential Market

Placentia's housing spans early ranch-era homes tied to its citrus heritage, extensive 1960s and 1970s tract neighborhoods, and pockets of newer construction, with historic sites such as the Bradford House and George Key Ranch reflecting the city's agricultural past. Values are influenced by the well-regarded Placentia-Yorba Linda school district, lot size, and the distinction between older near-downtown homes and later suburban subdivisions. The city's central north-county position and access to the 57 freeway sustain steady demand. For a retrospective estate valuation, aligning the subject with sales from the same neighborhood generation as of the date of death produces the most reliable result.

How much does a date-of-death appraisal cost in Placentia?

How long does a Placentia date-of-death appraisal take?

Do you need to visit the property in Placentia?

No. We reconstruct value from MLS records, public data, and market analysis specific to Placentia and its Orange County submarket, with no physical inspection required. This desktop approach is accepted by the IRS and the courts for estate purposes.

Can a desktop appraisal be used for IRS estate purposes in Placentia?

Yes. A desktop appraisal prepared by a California Certified Residential Appraiser to USPAP standards is accepted by the IRS for estate tax filings, stepped-up basis documentation, and Form 706 submissions.

What if the date of death in Placentia was years ago?

Retrospective appraisals with effective dates well in the past are routine. Historical MLS data and public records let us establish fair market value for a Placentia property as of a specific prior date with confidence.
